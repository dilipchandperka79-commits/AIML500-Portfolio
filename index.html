Artifact Visual
Title — What is it?

Generative AI Practice Documentation

This artifact is a documented AI practice lab based on a realistic healthcare communication scenario. The same patient-discharge prompt was tested in ChatGPT, Google Gemini, and Claude. The project also included Custom GPT refinement and STORM AI research-assistant practice.

Objective — Why did you create it?

Evaluate generative AI tools and practice responsible use

I created this artifact to understand how different Large Language Models respond to the same prompt, compare their strengths and limitations, improve my prompt engineering skills, and demonstrate why human review is necessary when AI is used for sensitive communication.

Process — How did you make it?

Prompt, test, compare, refine, research, and review

  1. Selected a patient-discharge communication problem involving high blood pressure treatment.
  2. Created a structured prompt using context, task requirements, and constraints.
  3. Tested the same prompt separately in ChatGPT, Google Gemini, and Claude.
  4. Compared clarity, organization, completeness, safety, usefulness, and tone.
  5. Used a Custom GPT to improve the discharge guide through multiple revisions.
  6. Used STORM AI to generate a structured research article and review its brainstorming process.
  7. Documented the findings, limitations, ethical considerations, and human-in-the-loop lesson.
Tools — Technology used

Generative AI, research, and documentation tools

ChatGPT Google Gemini Claude Custom GPT STORM AI Microsoft Word Prompt Engineering Human Review
Value — Artifact-Specific Value Proposition

A practical demonstration of AI evaluation and responsible implementation

This artifact helps students, software professionals, and organizational users understand that different AI models may provide different strengths even when they receive the same prompt. It demonstrates my ability to design prompts, compare AI output, improve a response through conversation, organize research, identify safety concerns, and use human judgment before applying AI-generated information in a real setting.

Artifact 2: AI in IT—Opportunities, Risks, and Human Impact

AI applications, enterprise platforms, real-life scenarios, risks, and governance

AIML-500-01N1D: Machine Learning Fundamentals
Artifact Visual
Title — What is it?

AI in IT: Opportunities, Risks, and Human Impact

This artifact is a graduate-level graphical presentation explaining how Artificial Intelligence is changing software development, cybersecurity, cloud and DevOps, service management, data analytics, infrastructure, testing, and IT governance.

Objective — Why did you create it?

Connect AI concepts with practical IT work

I created this presentation to explain where AI creates value in Information Technology, identify drawbacks that organizations must manage, examine its effect on employees and required skills, and show why responsible governance is important when AI is integrated into enterprise systems.

Process — How did you make it?

Research, organize, visualize, and evaluate

  1. Narrowed the topic from a broad AI timeline to the practical application of AI in IT.
  2. Identified eight IT fields affected by AI and mapped common use cases for each field.
  3. Reviewed enterprise platforms and models, including OpenAI, Microsoft, Google, Anthropic, AWS, and Meta.
  4. Developed real-life scenarios involving AI-assisted incident response and software development.
  5. Balanced benefits with risks such as hallucinations, data leakage, bias, over-reliance, security misuse, and job anxiety.
  6. Added human-centered governance recommendations, including approval controls, audit trails, testing, monitoring, and rollback.
  7. Designed the final PowerPoint with visual workflows, findings, scenarios, and APA-style references.
Tools — Technology used

Presentation, AI, and research tools

Microsoft PowerPoint ChatGPT Generative AI Large Language Models AIOps AI Governance Research Synthesis APA References
Value — Artifact-Specific Value Proposition

A balanced resource for responsible AI adoption in IT

This artifact gives IT students, software professionals, and organizational leaders a practical overview of how AI can improve software delivery, cybersecurity, operations, support, and decision-making while also creating privacy, security, quality, and workforce risks. It demonstrates my ability to translate emerging AI developments into understandable workplace scenarios and responsible implementation guidance.
